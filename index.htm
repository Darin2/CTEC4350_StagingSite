Project Summary

For this project, Something Visionary will be creating a new revamped website for The G.U.L.F. There are various areas that could be enhanced as the website currently stands. The G.U.L.F has expanded its services and goals; however, the website does not accurately communicate those additions to its visitors.

This redesign will address all the current problems by:

  1. Providing a smooth browsing experience with updated information about services to inform visitors and ultimately encourage recruitment
  2. Employing Search Engine Optimization (SEO) marketing strategies to increase organic traffic to the website
  3. Developing an enhanced brand identity to help with branding

With everything being increasingly virtualized, a well-designed website is essential. Good web design serves as a guide for your visitors and sets the first impression. The aforementioned improvements will help The G.U.L.F stand out from competitors, build trust with audiences, and improve SEO ranking.

Guidelines for Perception/Tone of the Website

Since The G.U.L.F’s mission is to inspire and empower their audience, who are mainly young adults, it will be most effective to adopt a friendly and motivating tone of voice throughout the website. Showcasing the positive impact of The G.U.L.F will be a high priority.

Intended Audience
The G.U.L.F’s intended audience consists of the underserved and uncertain, along with entrepreneur-minded youth and young adults. Potential sponsors and supporters are also part of the intended audience.
When underserved, uncertain or entrepreneurship-minded youth interact with The G.U.L.F’s website, our goal is to communicate that The G.U.L.F offers a program that will resonate with them and ignite their inspiration and drive.
When potential partners interact with The G.U.L.F’s website, our goal is to showcase The G.U.L.F’s positive impact on the local community and inspire these potential partners to get involved with The G.U.L.F.
Competitive Positioning
Although The G.U.L.F is not a for-profit entity, it still faces some small degree of competition for resources such as sponsorships and candidates. It could be beneficial to highlight the things that set The G.U.L.F apart from other similar entities, as long as this is done with a positive, friendly tone. Highlighting the “Diamond League” and the Youth Business Apprenticeship Program could be viable options in this area.
Targeted Messaging
Each of the intended audiences will benefit from targeted messaging that is intended specifically for them. Separate email lists can be created to easily target each group.

Audience Group: Participants

Participants are likely under the age of 18, attending high school or junior high school in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex. Participants may be of any gender. Participants will come from varying religious, socioeconomic and ethnic backgrounds.
Story

Our participant hears about The G.U.L.F through a family friend or a classmate, or via social media. The participant searches for information about The G.U.L.F via social media and Google. They visit the website and want to learn more about what opportunities The G.U.L.F has in store for them, and how to get involved.They may show The G.U.L.F’s site to their parents or guardians.
Interests, needs and wants

The participant will be interested in learning and growing as a young adult. They may be curious about leadership or how entrepreneurship works. They may be seeking like-minded young entrepreneurs, a role model, or an opportunity to learn marketable skills.
Technical Capability

The technical capability of participants may vary widely, from not very tech-savvy to extremely tech-savvy. On average, a participant will likely have higher technical capability than other user groups. Participants may not need as much instruction or guidance for technical tasks. Clear, succinct instructions and guides around technical topics will still be helpful.

Original G.U.L.F. Site Structure

Proposed Site Structure

The proposed G.U.L.F site contains a mostly flat structure of eight webpages, connected via links in the navigation bar. A secure link for administrator login will be provided at the bottom of the page. The pages will be: home, sign in, about, programs, get involved, challenges, admin control panel, and contact. The bottom of each page used by the public will contain a section featuring The G.U.L.F’s contact information along with a RingCentral link. Each page will have a highly visible donate button placed at the top of the page. All pages will be integrated with RingCentral to allow its use while on the site (see wireframes section for examples.)

All pages will be integrated with RingCentral to allow its use while on the site (see wireframes section for examples.)


Home

The Home page will introduce users to The G.U.L.F and offer multiple call-to-action buttons encouraging users to get involved. The home page will feature sections that encourage the user to get involved, show testimonials, showcase The G.U.L.F’s impact using statistics, and offer a Google Maps view of The G.U.L.F’s location.


Admin

The Admin page will let authenticated administrators access the admin control panel, which will contain a table featuring the contact information captured from the registration form and contact form.


About

The About page will feature several sections explaining The G.U.L.F’s mission, its impact on the community, and its story. Each section will feature a “call to action” button encouraging the user to get involved and linking to the Get Involved page.


Get Involved

The Get Involved page will attract participants and volunteers by explaining how to get involved with The G.U.L.F and directing users to the Contact page.


Challenge

The Challenge page will feature a monthly challenge for young entrepreneurs. This page will update automatically once per month, rotating through 12 challenges designed to stimulate the creativity and entrepreneurial capabilities of young people. This page was an idea offered by one of our teammates and is entirely optional.


Contact

The Contact page will feature contact information for The G.U.L.F staff, a form for questions or comments, and a sign up form for any current or future newsletters. This page will capture the user’s contact information in a database for later retrieval by the administrators.
